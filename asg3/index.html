By framing your project in a storytelling format, you provide yourself with a clearly defined, beginning, middle & end allowing you to bounce off of one act to the next. For example when I was pitching my semester project idea, I used the beginning to establish the background of my project that being centered around the game Final Fantasy XIV. Then I used the middle portion to explain the problem I want to address that being the lack of a comprehensive lore guide for the game. Finally I end the 3rd act by providing the intended solution to this problem that being creating a visual wiki that provides the user with the information they need.

Using bolder & larger font sizes can help draw the viewer to important parts of your website.

Creative Brief

Problem - Final Fantasy XIV lacks an system to keep track of lore.

Audience - My core audience will be players who play FFXIV.

Competition - Other wiki like sites centered around FFXIV.

Call to Action - When the target audience sees my website they will have a better understanding of the game's world building.
